**Application downtime** is the period of time when an application (website, API, or system) is **not available or not functioning properly for users**.

***

# 🧠 Simple Definition

> **Downtime = Time when users cannot access or use the application as expected**

***

# 🔍 Types of Downtime

### 1. **Planned Downtime**

*   Happens intentionally (e.g., during deployments, maintenance)
*   Example: “App will be unavailable from 2 AM–3 AM for updates”

✅ Controlled and expected

***

### 2. **Unplanned Downtime**

*   Happens due to unexpected issues
*   Examples:
    *   Server crash
    *   Network failure
    *   Bug in deployment
    *   Database outage

❌ Risky and often costly

***

# ⚠️ What Counts as Downtime?

*   Website not loading at all ❌
*   APIs returning errors (500, 503) ❌
*   Extremely slow responses ❌
*   Features not working properly ❌

Even **partial failure** is often considered downtime.

***

# 📊 Why Downtime Matters

### 🚨 Impact:

*   Lost revenue (for businesses)
*   Poor user experience
*   Damage to reputation
*   SLA (Service Level Agreement) violations

***

# ⏱️ Example

Suppose:

*   Your app runs 24 hours/day
*   It goes down for 1 hour

👉 Downtime = **1 hour**

👉 Availability = **(23 / 24) × 100 = 95.8%**

***

# ✅ How Deployment Strategies Handle Downtime

| Strategy   | Downtime Impact |
| ---------- | --------------- |
| Recreate   | ❌ High downtime |
| Rolling    | ✅ Low downtime  |
| Blue-Green | ✅ No downtime   |
| Canary     | ✅ No downtime   |

***

# 🧠 Key Concept

*   **High availability systems aim for “zero downtime”**
*   Often measured using **uptime % (e.g., 99.9%, 99.99%)**
