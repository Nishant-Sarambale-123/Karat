If production goes down, what steps do you take?


In a production-down scenario, I follow a structured and calm approach to quickly restore service while minimizing impact. First, I acknowledge the incident and assess its severity by checking alerts, monitoring dashboards, and logs to understand the scope and user impact. I immediately inform stakeholders and, if needed, join or initiate an incident bridge to coordinate efforts. Then, I start with basic health checks—verifying service availability, dependent systems like databases or third-party APIs, recent deployments, and infrastructure status. If the issue correlates with a recent release, I prioritize a rollback or feature toggle to bring the system back to a stable state. Parallelly, I analyze logs and metrics to identify the root cause, whether it’s a code issue, configuration problem, infrastructure failure, or traffic spike.

Once the service is restored, I ensure stability by monitoring key metrics closely. After that, I conduct a thorough root cause analysis and document the incident, including timeline, impact, and resolution steps. I also work on preventive measures such as adding better monitoring, alerts, test coverage, or improving deployment strategies. Finally, I share learnings with the team through a postmortem to avoid similar incidents in the future.
