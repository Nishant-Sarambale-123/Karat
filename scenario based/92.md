Which deployment strategy fits small-scale industry

For small-scale industries, the most suitable deployment strategy is **Recreate deployment** or sometimes **Rolling deployment** depending on the need.

👉 In simple interview terms:  
For small-scale applications, **Recreate strategy** works well because it is simple to implement and manage. In this approach, the old version of the application is stopped first, and then the new version is deployed. It may cause a short downtime, but for small systems with low traffic, this is usually acceptable. If downtime needs to be avoided, a **Rolling update** can be used, where the old version is gradually replaced with the new version without stopping the entire application.

✅ So:

*   **Recreate** → simple, cost-effective (best for small scale)
*   **Rolling update** → no downtime, slightly more advanced

***

## 🎯 Short Interview Answer

"For small-scale industries, the Recreate deployment strategy is commonly used because it is simple and easy to manage, even though it causes brief downtime. If zero downtime is required, a rolling update strategy can be used to gradually replace old versions with new ones."
