What deployment strategies to use in CI/CD and when
**Interview-Style Answer:**

“In CI/CD, different deployment strategies are used depending on the risk tolerance, system criticality, and need for zero downtime. The most common strategies are Blue-Green, Canary, Rolling, and Recreate deployment.

For example, **Recreate deployment** is the simplest approach where we stop the old version and deploy the new one. It’s suitable for non-critical applications where downtime is acceptable.

**Rolling deployment** gradually replaces instances of the old version with the new version. It is useful for applications that need high availability, as there is no complete downtime, but there is a brief period where both versions coexist.

**Blue-Green deployment** involves maintaining two identical environments—one active (blue) and one idle (green). The new version is deployed to the idle environment and then traffic is switched. This is ideal for minimizing downtime and enabling quick rollback, especially in production-critical systems.

**Canary deployment** releases the new version to a small subset of users first. Based on monitoring and feedback, it is gradually rolled out to all users. This strategy is best for high-risk changes where you want to validate performance and stability before full release.

Additionally, **feature flags** are often used along with these strategies to control feature rollout without redeployment.

So, the choice of deployment strategy depends on factors like downtime tolerance, risk level, user impact, and rollback requirements. For example, I would use Blue-Green or Canary for production systems requiring zero downtime, and Rolling for general updates, while Recreate can be used in lower environments or less critical services.”
