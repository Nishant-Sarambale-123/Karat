How to ensure pods are healthy and ready to serve traffic?

Here’s a **strong interview-style answer**:

***

**“To ensure that pods are healthy and ready to serve traffic in Kubernetes, I primarily rely on liveness and readiness probes along with proper monitoring and resource management. Readiness probes are used to determine when a pod is ready to accept traffic; until the probe succeeds, Kubernetes does not route any requests to that pod. Liveness probes, on the other hand, check if the application inside the pod is still running correctly, and if it fails, Kubernetes automatically restarts the container to maintain health.”**

**“In addition to probes, I verify pod status using commands like `kubectl get pods` and `kubectl describe pod` to check conditions such as Ready and ContainersReady. I also ensure proper resource requests and limits are defined to avoid crashes due to resource starvation. Monitoring tools like Prometheus and Grafana help track application health metrics, while logs (`kubectl logs`) provide insights into runtime issues. Together, these practices ensure that pods remain healthy, automatically recover from failures, and only receive traffic when they are fully ready.”**

***

### ✅ Key Commands (quick mention in interview)

```bash
kubectl get pods
kubectl describe pod <pod-name>
kubectl logs <pod-name>
```

***

### 🎯 Closing Line:

**“So, by combining readiness probes, liveness probes, proper resource configuration, and monitoring, we can ensure pods are both healthy and capable of serving traffic reliably.”**
